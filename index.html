Mt. Fuji 3D2N FUGAKU Stay and Hokusai Ecotour Package

Have you ever seen ukiyoe paintings by one of the most world famous artists, Katsushika Hokusai and wished to view Mt. Fuji like him someday in Japan? Actually, our local town, Fujinomiya, located at the western foot of Mt. Fuji, is where many of the famous 36 Views of Mount Fuji and 100 Views of Mount Fuji were painted. In addition, unlike the Fuji Five Lakes and Hakone, where many tourists visit, our area still retains its authentic culture and ancient landscape. Visitors can walk through different villages, shrines and temples, view Mt. Fuji like Hokusai and draw Mt. Fuji with a Japanese brush (筆)like Hokusai. You can also enjoy staying the Hokusai-themed Japanese private house in the middle of a village by Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park. There are many walking trails around the guesthouse, so this ecotour package is perfect for nature and culture walking lovers!

Glamping and Ecotour Packages

Enjoy nature and culture ecotours by local English-speaking guides in and around Mt. Fuji staying at private glamping site (only for two small groups) seeing majestic views of Mt. Fuji. Early morning nature walks are available everyday in Mt. Fuji National Park. You can select one full-day ecotour from Village Culture E-bike, Nature E-bike, Culture Walk, Forest Walk, Farm-to-Table Tours, etc.

Private Mt. Fuji Day Ecotours

Exploring Mt Fuji by E-bike in this unique one-day ecotour. Visit Lake Tanuki National Park, ride through a traditional Japanese village, see falls and try fresh spring water from Mt Fuji. Enjoy fresh rainbow trout lunch at a local trout-farm and then ride to the World Heritage Shiraito falls. Finish the day at our glamping site with a local craft beer sounded by paddy field in front of Mt. Fuji!

③Fujinomiya Township and Culture Ecotour

Fujinomiya City is the gateway to Mt. Fuji with the main Sengen shrine, a UNESCO World Heritage site, which was built for the sole purpose of worshiping and showing respect to this iconic mountain. Visit a traditional craftsman and learn how to sharpen Japanese cooking knives. Get dressed in a kimono at the local kimono shop, walk to the shrine with a local English-speaking guide and learn about the history and local rituals that have taken place here for centuries. Enjoy authentic local food, including Fujinomiya’s famous Yakisoba. At the end of the tour, learn how to enjoy Shizuoka green tea by the owner of the tea farm with the Japanese sweet.

⑤Mt. Fuji Hiking and Shrine Ecotour
